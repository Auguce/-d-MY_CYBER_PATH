Key Project: Key Exchange using Neural Networks

Honours Year Computer Science Project, University of Liverpool, UK
(Sept 2022 - Jun 2023)

This project delved into the innovative Kanter-Kinzel-Kanter (K-K-K) protocol, which employs neural networks for secure key exchange. Under the guidance of Professor Alexei Lisitsa, I explored the theoretical foundations of the protocol, analyzed its resilience to attacks, and developed a Python-based prototype to simulate its operation in real-world scenarios.

My work included:

  • Designing a neural network structure with defined weight update rules to implement the K-K-K protocol.
  • Building and testing synchronization mechanisms between communication parties (Alice and Bob).
  • Simulating and evaluating the protocol’s robustness against genetic algorithm-based attacks.
  • Analyzing how different parameters affected synchronization speed and the likelihood of successful attacks.

Despite some limitations in fully implementing advanced attack methods within the project timeline, the research highlighted the potential of neural networks in secure communication while identifying areas for further optimization and future exploration.

Graduate-Level Cybersecurity Course, Boston University, USA

The scanner addressed limitations in existing tools like Fullhunt by introducing innovative features such as third-party service analysis and advanced payload customization.

Features:

My Contribution: Focused on URL scraping, scanner enhancements, and bug fixes to ensure reliable detection and comprehensive reporting.

Key Project: Web3 Game Development using Blockchain

Graduate-Level Blockchain Application Project, Boston University, USA
(Feb 2024 - Jun 2024)

This project explored the innovative integration of blockchain technology into a 2D RPG (Role-Playing Game) format, enabling true digital ownership of in-game assets through NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) and a decentralized economy. As part of a team, we developed Poket Mine, a game designed to combine engaging gameplay with educational insights into blockchain and NFT technology.

Project Highlights:

  • Developed a dynamic NFT-based battle system, where players used computational hash power to influence gameplay outcomes.
  • Designed smart contracts using Solidity for Ethereum blockchain, implementing ERC721 standards for NFTs.
  • Integrated blockchain functions directly into game mechanics using Web3.js, enabling seamless interactions like NFT minting, trading, and management.
  • Built a frontend interface using HTML and Web3.js, with MetaMask integration for secure user authentication and transaction handling.

Game Features

  • True Digital Ownership: Players own in-game assets as NFTs, allowing trading and verified ownership via blockchain.
  • Dynamic Gameplay: Attributes of NFTs, like level and Ether binding, dynamically adjust based on user actions and game mechanics.
  • Mining and Battles: Players contribute computational hash power in a mining pool for training and battles, earning rewards based on contributions and outcomes.
  • Community Economy: The decentralized economy empowers players to shape the marketplace, influencing the rarity and value of NFT assets.
  • Educational Insights: The game demystifies blockchain concepts, teaching players the potential of NFTs beyond collectibles and scams.

My Contribution

As a key team member, I focused on smart contract programming and functionality design. My responsibilities included:

  • Writing and deploying Solidity smart contracts to manage NFT minting, trading, and dynamic attribute adjustments.
  • Developing features to securely handle Ether transactions and integrate blockchain-based property transfers.
  • Enhancing the scanner’s detection of third-party blockchain components embedded in game scripts.
  • Debugging and testing to ensure seamless interaction between the frontend interface and Ethereum blockchain.

Reflection

This project was a transformative experience that deepened my understanding of blockchain's applications in gaming. By integrating NFTs and a decentralized economy into gameplay, we demonstrated the potential of blockchain technology to redefine ownership and interaction in digital ecosystems. This experience has strengthened my interest in exploring Web3 development and its applications in emerging technologies.

Key Project: Computer Network Programming Assignments

Graduate-Level Computer Networking Course, Boston University, USA
(Sept 2024 - Dec 2024)

This course provided an in-depth understanding of network protocols and their implementations. I designed and implemented transport protocols and network applications, focusing on TCP performance measurement and reliable data transfer mechanisms.

Assignment 1: TCP Performance Measurement

Objective: Measure and analyze TCP performance under various conditions, focusing on throughput, congestion, and retransmissions.

Key Tasks:

  • Developed a client-server application to simulate TCP data transfer and record performance metrics such as RTT, throughput, and congestion window size.
  • Implemented multiple congestion control mechanisms (e.g., Tahoe, Reno) and compared their behaviors under different network conditions.
  • Simulated packet loss, delay, and jitter to analyze their impact on TCP performance and visualized results using Python's matplotlib.

Reflection: This assignment enhanced my understanding of TCP's congestion control mechanisms and their behavior in real-world scenarios. It provided practical experience in network simulation and performance evaluation.

Assignment 2: Reliable Data Transfer Protocols

Objective: Design and implement reliable data transfer protocols using Selective-Repeat and Go-Back-N mechanisms in a simulated network environment.

Key Tasks:

  • Developed a transport-layer protocol simulation with packet loss and delay.
  • Implemented Go-Back-N and Selective-Repeat algorithms for reliable data transfer, ensuring packet delivery even under challenging network conditions.
  • Simulated scenarios with varying window sizes and packet loss rates to evaluate protocol efficiency and performance.
  • Visualized protocol performance metrics (e.g., retransmissions, throughput) to compare their effectiveness.

Reflection: Through this assignment, I gained a deep understanding of reliable data transfer protocols, their strengths, and their limitations. This hands-on experience strengthened my programming skills and my ability to design efficient networking solutions.

Conclusion: These programming assignments were instrumental in solidifying my knowledge of computer networking concepts and protocols. The practical implementations allowed me to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and real-world applications.

Key Course: Enterprise Cybersecurity Management

Graduate-Level Cybersecurity Management Course, Boston University, USA
(Sept 2024 - Dec 2024)

The Enterprise Cybersecurity Management course was a transformative experience that deepened my understanding of managing cybersecurity and privacy programs in dynamic and complex environments. Guided by experienced instructors and through engaging discussions, assignments, and case studies, I gained a nuanced perspective on how effective cybersecurity management is built upon strong governance, robust policies, and strategic alignment with organizational goals.

Key Learnings and Takeaways

  • Governance and Policy Frameworks: Gained insight into developing and evaluating policies, including the creation of a Written Information Security Program (WISP), and connected global standards like NIST and ISO 27001 to real-world implementations.
  • Risk Management Fundamentals: Learned to systematically identify, quantify, and mitigate risks, while balancing security measures with organizational risk tolerance and business priorities.
  • Asset Classification and Intellectual Property Protection: Acquired practical techniques for assessing asset value, particularly in scenarios involving sensitive information, to safeguard critical resources.
  • Incident Response and Disaster Recovery Planning: Developed an incident response plan and structured recovery processes to minimize damage and ensure business continuity. Practiced postmortem reviews for continuous improvement.
  • Alignment with Business Goals: Understood how to align cybersecurity initiatives with organizational objectives, ensuring security measures not only protect but also empower the organization.

Personal Growth and Skills Development

Through this course, I developed:

  • Strong technical writing and documentation skills, particularly in creating security policies and risk assessments.
  • Enhanced communication and collaboration skills, fostered through in-depth discussions and group activities.
  • A holistic understanding of cybersecurity management, bridging technical solutions, governance, and organizational strategy.

Reflection and Future Applications

The Enterprise Cybersecurity Management course not only equipped me with practical tools and knowledge but also reshaped my perspective on the broader role of cybersecurity in modern organizations. As I move forward in my career, I am confident that the skills and insights gained from this course will enable me to lead cybersecurity initiatives effectively, ensuring that security measures not only protect but also empower the organizations I serve.
